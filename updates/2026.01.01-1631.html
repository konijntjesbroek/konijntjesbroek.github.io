**NEW PROJECT** Unnamed Studio Project

As a way of connecting with my mother, I am diving back into to art she wanted me to pursue that more fully when I was younger, but I mostly blew it off witn younger man's concerns. Money, stuff, the usual. I have dabbled with artwork over the intervening years, but never really set about to learn the deeper side of formal study of forms and manipulation since I was in high school. I have bought a lot of books on the subject but never make it past the first few pages. Recently, while watching some autodidactic videos on YouTube, I came across the the following quote, which really resonated with me.

The true learner obsesses over the process, they want to learn every aspect that will allow them to succeed.
This made me reconsider what I have doing.

Historically, I pick things up pretty easy, and if I don't I move on. While I have been able to skate by on this methodology for a large portion of my life, it has hindered me in ways I had't realized or more accurately in ways I didn't want to admit. I just preferred to think of myself has having a broad understanding that allowed me to join into multiple conversations, and because of my quick wit and solid research acumen, I could know more, quicker than about 80-90% of the population when I needed to. I don't want to just be better than most folks with surface knowledge any more. I want to understand and be able to relay that knowledge effectively to others. To pursue this I have identified a few key regions I want formally study:

  1. Pen and Ink Sketching/Drawing
  2. Watercolor Painting
  3. Penmanship/Calligraphy

for now I am pushing this to instagram to get get some digital presence until I get some work that I feel is decent. I will then start figuring out a place to make prints available at a reasonable price. Pricing on originals will be according to the time I put in at a rate of 50-100/hour + materials.
