Welcome to the Arlonian Institute of Useless Information

I have been using this grouping as a catchall for my random ideas that help me to see the world a little differently. Initially, I used it to produce 1st hand research doccuments on papers I created in the mid-90s. Later I moved on to structuring random groupthink and and human thought processing experiments. About this time I started to get involved in the open software and linux movements.

Most of these projects are half finished, but in the process of building out a load of half done projects I learned a good bit about what works for prepping a project. I have saved that knowledge under the layout templates. I hope you enjoy the site and if you feel like I might fit in with something you are working on, drop me a line

The Color Scheme

This is designed to mimic the amber screened monitors I first started learning on. The University of Memphis math lab gave me access to vax/vms, gopher, nexus/lexus and irc. It also allowed me to start playinjg with html and c. I stayed too long and missed my ride.

The Font

I suffer from floaty word/number syndrome, especially when I get tired. This font decreases the number of re-reads/jumps by at least 2-3 times over what standard sans fonts do. I cannot recommend this enough it you struggle with the same.

The Glider

The glider was designated by Eric Raymond to be the universal identifiers for those of us driven more by curiosity than self preservation. It is the first self propulsive form discovered in the Game of Life I have implemented my own version, listed on the projects page.
