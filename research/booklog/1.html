The lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe

Author:C.S. Lewis
Publication: 1950
Completion dates:
  • 2026-01-06

Summary:

Four children are sent to the country to live with a professor during the bombing of London in WWII. While playing a round the peculiarly unique grounds, Lucy, the youngest, stumbles onto a fantastic befriends a faun who tells her of the white witch, her curse on Narnia that cause an endless winter with no Christmas. After returning through the wardrobe to find no time had passed, Lucy's siblings, especially Edmund accuse her of telling falsehoods.

After checking the wardrobe repeatedly, eventually Lucy finds her way back to Narnia, and this time Edmund follows. While Lucy is having a tea with Tumnus, Edmund meets the Queen of Narnia and gorges on Turkish Delight. He also vows to return, bringing his siblings with him. On returning from Narnia, Edmund tells the others they were only playing make believe.

Concerned by the story spinning from Lucy, Peter and Susan go to the professor for guidance. He asks which is more believable, Lucy is relentlessly asserting that she is visiting a far off land in the face of obviously no time passing, when historically she has been honest and loyal, or that it is actually happening?

A while later all four children are caught off guard when the stern Mrs. McCready is giving a tour of the manor and they seek shelter in the wardrobe. They find themselves in a bleak Narnia instead. Tumnus has been taken by the White Witch's police chief Fenrir Ulf. The children are guided by a robin to Mr. Beaver who takes them to his dam and feeds them. He then tells them of Aslan's return and the fate of poor Mr. Tumnus. During the telling, Edmund sneaks off to the Queen and the remaining party makes a hasty retreat toward the stone table.

The next morning, the Beavers and the children are visited by Father Christmas and given gifts. Peter: Sword and Shield; Lucy: A healing tonic; Susan: a bow and quiver which cannot miss. Throughout the day, the winter fades and spring blooms. This slows the White Witch's progress. Edmund realizes the error of his way. The other children are able to reach the stone table.

The White Whitch claims Edmund for his treachery and that he must be killed on the stone table according to the old magic. Aslan offers himself as a sacrifice in Edmund's place, and moves the gathering army to safety coaching Peter in tactics along the way. After camp is set up, Aslan sets off, lonely and sad to his death. Lucy and Susan follow, not knowing he is dying for Edmund. They are told to wait a ways off and witness the cowardice and creulty of the witch's army but hold back as Aslan asked. After Aslan is bound, shorn and slaughtered, her army sets out to Aslan's Army's encampment.

The girls unmuzzle Aslan and mice come to chew his bindings off. As the girls sit mourning throughout the night they barely notice the other is there. As the sun is rising, the stone table splits and Aslan appears. He explains the deeper magic from before time began. They set off to the witch's home and reanimate the creatures that she had turned to stone. The party then joins the remaining army and Aslan defeats the witch.

The children rule Narnia for many years and decide to chase a white stag that is said to grant a wish if caught. They track it to the long forgotten wood that leads to the wardrobe, to find no time has passed.
